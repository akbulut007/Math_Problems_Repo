# Task 2 — Rolling a Die

# Problem

A fair die is rolled.

Describe the sample space for:

• one roll  
• two rolls  
• three rolls

---

# Step 1 — One die roll

Possible outcomes:

Ω₁ = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₁| = 6

---

# Step 2 — Two die rolls

We record the results in order.

Examples of outcomes:

(1,1)  
(1,2)  
(2,5)  
(6,6)

Sample space:

Ω₂ = {(i,j) : i,j ∈ {1,2,3,4,5,6}}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₂| = 6 · 6 = 36

---

# Step 3 — Three die rolls

We record the results in order.

Examples of outcomes:

(1,1,1)  
(2,4,6)  
(6,6,6)

Sample space:

Ω₃ = {(i,j,k) : i,j,k ∈ {1,2,3,4,5,6}}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₃| = 6 · 6 · 6 = 6³ = 216

---

# Final Result

|Ω₁| = 6  
|Ω₂| = 36  
|Ω₃| = 216

For **n rolls** the number of outcomes is:

6ⁿ

---

# Quick intuition

```text
1 roll
 1  2  3  4  5  6

2 rolls
 first roll  -> 6 choices
 second roll -> 6 choices
 total       -> 6 × 6 = 36

3 rolls
 total -> 6 × 6 × 6 = 216
```
