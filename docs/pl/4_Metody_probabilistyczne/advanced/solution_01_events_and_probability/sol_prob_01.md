# Task 1 — Coin Tossing

# Problem

A fair coin is tossed.

Let

H — heads  
T — tails

Describe the sample space for:

• one toss  
• two tosses  
• three tosses

---

# Step 1 — One coin toss

Possible outcomes:

Ω₁ = {H, T}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₁| = 2

---

# Step 2 — Two coin tosses

We record the results in order.

Possible outcomes:

(H,H)  
(H,T)  
(T,H)  
(T,T)

Sample space:

Ω₂ = {(H,H), (H,T), (T,H), (T,T)}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₂| = 4

---

# Step 3 — Three coin tosses

Possible outcomes:

HHH  
HHT  
HTH  
HTT  
THH  
THT  
TTH  
TTT

Sample space:

Ω₃ = {HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT}

Number of outcomes:

|Ω₃| = 8

---

# Final Result

|Ω₁| = 2  
|Ω₂| = 4  
|Ω₃| = 8

For **n tosses** the number of outcomes is:

2ⁿ

---

# Quick intuition

```text
1 toss
 H
 T

2 tosses
 H → H
   → T
 T → H
   → T
```
